Linux 101

The "Linux 101" session, led by Mohammed Nouman and organized by Glug Pace, taught second-year Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) students about Linux. Nouman explained Linux as a free operating system with benefits like flexibility, security, and strong community support. He then talked about how operating systems work, which is important for managing software and hardware in computers. He also discussed dual booting, where you can run two operating systems at once, covering topics like file systems, partitions, and swap systems. Additionally, attendees learned about different versions of Linux, showing how customizable it is. Overall, the session gave students a good start in understanding Linux, helping them use it in their studies and careers. Special thanks to Nouman and the organizers for making the session informative and relevant to CSE students.
