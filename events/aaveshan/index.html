Aaveshan: Introduction of Clubs to First Years

Date: 31-10-2023

Venue: Multipurpose Hall

The GLUG PACE (GNU/Linux User Group - PACE) organized a series of sessions targeting the first year and second year students of P A College of Engineering.This session aims to ignite our juniors, showcasing the joy and excitement of embracing the geek culture getting ready to dive into the world of tech and fun. We showed them how we had a blast learning and having fun at workshops, tech events, hackathons, and competitions. It's all about enjoying the journey while aiming for success.

"This was GLUG's first Aveshan event, ultimately started for first-year students. However, we expanded it to include both first and second-year batches for this time."

This session also gave an introduction to various clubs for first-year students. The event aimed to inspire and guide incoming students on their academic journey and encourage their active participation in clubs and activities. The event featured five speakers, each sharing their inspiring stories.

Nouman commenced the session by sharing his personal experiences and introducing the students to the various clubs at PACE. He discussed his participation in hackathons, highlighting the challenges he faced and the valuable knowledge he gained, especially in Linux. Nouman emphasized the importance of learning from these experiences and how they ultimately led to his successful placement at Novigo Solutions.

Muadh, a bio student with a passion for computer science, followed, sharing his journey of overcoming challenges in the field. He motivated students to embrace new learning opportunities and discussed his active involvement in club activities, his ability to stay in touch with seniors for guidance, and how these connections enhanced his academic journey.

Saniya then took the stage to describe her transformation from being an introvert to becoming a social and competitive participant in college activities. She discussed her journey from not understanding code to learning Python and how her efforts led to internships and placement in her desired company.

Shabnaz described her introduction to the Embedded Club and how it opened doors to exciting opportunities. She detailed her participation in the ADC competition at IIIT, her victory, and how it led to a subsequent internship. Shabnaz also talked about how she became a GDSC Lead and stressed the importance of staying connected with teachers and seniors for guidance.She also encouraged the first-year students to embrace opportunities and not confine themselves within the classroom.

Shakeel shared his journey, starting from ground zero, to participating in inUnity,Sahyadri hack, achieving accolades, and pursuing his interest in cryptocurrencies and the stock market. He emphasized the importance of confidence and active participation in hackathons.

Rabeeh concluded the session by expressing gratitude to the senior students for their continuous support and guidance.

Conclusion:

The career guidance and club introduction session was a great success, providing invaluable insights and motivation to the first-year students at GLUG PACE. It not only inspired them to actively engage in various club activities but also encouraged them to face challenges with confidence and enthusiasm.
